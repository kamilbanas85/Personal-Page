IT SKILLS/PROJECTS

First contact with programming I had during math studies. I learned basic concepts of OOP based on Java programming language. Besides classic course in programing we also learned programming some algorithms such as such brute force or divide-and-conquer.

After graduation of Aeronautics and Space Technology I decided to develop myself in numerical computer-aided engineering (CAE) software development. It was great opportunity to connect my two profesions: math and engineering. CAE softwares are used to simulation of physiscal phenomena such as heat transfer, fluid flow or stress in material.

The most popular commercial CAE softwares were written few years ago in old-fashioned leangueages, such as Fortran or C. Moreover, that's softwares are very complex and any development are limited to writing user subroutine. One of the Fortran subroutine, which extended MSC.Marc software was written by me in 2015 during my Phd study. The subroutine was described in the paper:
K. Banas, Implementation of the nonlinear imperfect transmission conditions between dissimilar materials into commercial FEM software MSC.Marc using Fortran user subroutine: Plane strain case, Composite Interfaces, Vol. 22, No. 6, pp. 447-471.

In 2015, I changed a thesis of my Phd and I wrote next Fortran subroutine in 2017, which extended ANSYS Mechanical software. The code is available on my GitHub and it was described in the paper:
K. Banas and J. Badur, Influence of strength differential effect on material effort of a turbine guide vane based on thermoelastoplastic analysis, Journal of Thermal Stresses, Vol. 40, No. 11, pp. 1368-1385.

Then, during my scientific work at Insitute of Fluid flow Machineri PAS, I extended GUI of ANSYS Thermal software. I used XML and Phyton. The code is avaliable on my GitHub. The program interpolates data from CFD to thermal mesh and evaluetes heat transfer coefficents based on 3-points method. Finally, it sets up boundary conditions in solver input.

Now, I develop my skills in java.
